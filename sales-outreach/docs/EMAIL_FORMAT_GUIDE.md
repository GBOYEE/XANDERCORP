# XanderCorp Email Format Guide
> Custom Gmail layout for professional outreach — 2026 best practices

## Golden Rules

1. **40-60 words max** per email
2. **Subject: 2-4 words, personalized, no numbers**
3. **Lead with value, not pitch**
4. **No attachments in first email**
5. **Always test before sending**
6. **Filter leads before outreach**

---

## Email Structure (Clean, No Bloat)

```
Subject: {Personalized hook — 2-4 words}

Hi {firstName},

{Value-first message — 1-2 sentences max}
{Maybe a trigger or specific observation}
{Concrete offer or question}

{Soft CTA — one line max}

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
{Link to sales page OR portfolio}
```

## Bad vs Good Format

### ❌ BAD (Generic, Boring, No Hook)

```
Subject: AI Automation Services

Hi John,

I help businesses automate their workflows using AI. We offer
custom AI agents that can help streamline your operations and
save you time. Our services include lead generation, customer
support automation, and workflow optimization.

Would you be interested in a quick call to discuss how we can
help your business?

Best regards,
Oyebanji
```

### ✅ GOOD (Specific, Value-First, Personal)

```
Subject: quick question about {company}

Hi John,

Saw {company} recently {trigger event}. Most teams at your
stage struggle with {specific pain point}.

We help {similar companies} fix this — one client cut manual
work by 60% in the first week.

Worth a quick chat?

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
xandercorp.com
```

## 5 Winning Templates (2026 Data-Backed)

### Template 1: Value-First Audit
```
Subject: quick question about {company}

Hi {firstName},

I looked at {company}'s top landing pages. There are 3 specific
fixes that could improve conversion — I'll record a Loom walkthrough
if you're interested.

No pitch, just the audit. Worth it?

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
```

### Template 2: Trigger Event
```
Subject: congrats on the raise

Hi {firstName},

Saw {company} just {funding/product launch/hiring}. Most teams
at this stage start scaling outbound but hit data quality issues fast.

We helped {similar company} automate their outreach and cut manual
work by 40%.

Interested in how?

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
```

### Template 3: Pain Point (PAS)
```
Subject: {pain point} at {company}?

Hi {firstName},

Most {job title}s at {company size} companies tell us
{pain point} is eating 5+ hours a week.

We built a solution specifically for this. Takes 15 minutes
to see if it fits.

Worth a look?

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
```

### Template 4: Competitor Mention
```
Subject: how {competitor} handles this

Hi {firstName},

{Competitor} switched to AI automation last quarter and cut
their {metric} by {number}%. Thought you'd want to know what
they changed.

Happy to share the breakdown — no strings.

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
```

### Template 5: Quick Question
```
Subject: {firstName}, quick question

Hi {firstName},

Quick question — how are you currently handling {specific task}?

I built an automation for a {industry} company that handles
this in 2 minutes instead of 2 hours.

Can I show you how I'd approach it for {company}?

—
Oyebanji
Founder, XanderCorp
```

## Subject Line Rules

| Tactic | Open Rate |
|--------|-----------|
| Personalized subject | 46% |
| No personalization | 35% |
| Question format | 46% |
| 2-4 words | 46% |
| 9-10 words | 34% |
| Numbers in subject | 27% ❌ |
| Marketing hype (ASAP, etc) | <36% ❌ |

**Keep it short. Make it personal. Ask a question.**

## Pre-Send Checklist

Before ANY email goes out:

- [ ] Email under 60 words?
- [ ] Subject is 2-4 words, personalized?
- [ ] No numbers in subject?
- [ ] Lead with value, not pitch?
- [ ] Lead verified (not bounced)?
- [ ] Company researched (not generic)?
- [ ] Test email sent to self first?
- [ ] CEO approved before first batch?
